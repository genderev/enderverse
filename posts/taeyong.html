desire can make anything a god

When I met Taeyong it felt like I was alive for the first time. When he left me, it felt like all the life had been drained out of me.

I could go on about how Taeyong is the beauty Elaine Scarry talks about in On Beauty -

  • like a sun which it was somehow possible to stare at and which was coming nearer and nearer
  • people follow the paths of migrating birds and moving strangers to keep the beautiful thing present to them
  • the pliancy/elasticity of beauty is hurtling us forward and back, requiring us to break new ground
- but the problem is everyone who meets Taeyong tries to write their own poetry. They all try to describe their experience in this way and try to make sense of their encounter using figurative language.

Everyone understands this. Everyone has the same experience encountering Tayeong's aura which is never transmitted in the image of Taeyong that gets digitally reproduced. Taeyong's aura is lost in digital reproductions.

Experiencing his very strong aura that was so fresh and lively - the memory brings me to tears. It is this sweet aura that I long to encounter again. Ever since I met Taeyong and was enveloped by his sweet, fresh aura that made me feel so alive, I have been chasing that feeling and trying to feel that same high. I wonder who else has been chasing this high, too. Maybe we should meet each other.

The way that I cope with being separated from life itself - the sun which feeds the plants and the animals - is by taking walks in the park. There is one flower in particular that reminds me of Taeyong. It is sweet, but not saccharine. It is fresh, but not earthy. It is alive. When I brush my nose up against the petals I can see ants in the leaves. I feel the dirt edging into my shoes. I feel alive.

It is so painful to be separate from light itself - that source of light which allows life to continue. But I go to the park, and I smell the flowers, and it reminds me of him. And I understand.

I have to do this for the rest of my life. This is how I live. I have to do this.
