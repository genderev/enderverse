painting taeyong

possession in my "art"

I want to talk about painting - just quoting what I read in Ways Of Seeing from when I had enough free time to read books this summer.

Reasons fanart exists:

  • beauty creates copies of itself
  • get attention from other fans
  • get attention from subject of fanart
  • gain possession over the subject of the fanart ← our topic for today

I already described how photographers exercise power over the people being photographed. Every time you make art using the likeness of someone else, you are only describing your own worldview. A photographic portrait of someone is not objective reality, but merely the photographer's view of the person they photographed.

When I use neural style transfer and super-resolution convolutional neural networks to generate images that look like Taeyong, I can own some form of Taeyong's being, his likeness.

It is harder to be surrounded by books and pets than it is to be surrounded by images. The particular texture of the subject in a painting is so...carnal. The painting's viewer can reach into the painting and touch and feel, not just see. Hanging up a portrait of a gold necklace painted with gold leaf is almost as good as having the necklace itself.

“For Renaissance artists, painting was perhaps an instrument of knowledge but it was also an instrument of possession..."

Cameras show that the notion of time passing is part of the experience of the visual. In paintings, the subject is frozen in time.

Images are for consuming:

"As we make images and consume them, we need more."
and the delightful element about consuming images that are paintings/painting-like is the lack of mortality in the subject. Photographs show the subject at a certain point in time, pointing to life and death, but paintings are not so somber.

Given that I feel so embarrassed/stressed about my Taeyong obssession, I wonder if Tarkovsky is talking about me when he discusses artists in Sculpting in Time:

“True artistic inspiration is always a torment for the artist, almost to the point of endangering his life. Its realisation is tantamount to a physical feat."

(Technically, my "art" is kpop fanart?...no. I hate to admit that.)
