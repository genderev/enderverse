Why does Thousand Fell buy carbon offsets?

The Thousand Fell website admits that they buy carbon offsets.

Carbon offsets assign a monetary value to greenhouse gas pollution. This allows fossil fuel extraction to continue under the assumption that the global economic market will somehow force significant emissions reductions.

This belief has not come true, so why are carbon offsets used by Thousand Fell?

This is greenwashing. Carbon offsets can be purchased by polluters to claim the status of "being green" while still causing pollution. Pollution persists in communities disproportionately populated by people of color, and this is called environmental racism.

Researchers from the University of Leeds find that oil refineries are more likely to be in low-income US neighborhoods.

Asian, Black, and Latino communities fight asthma because they live near petroleum refineries. Indigneous people struggle aginst the creation of fracking pielines in their communities.

Is Thousand Fell zero waste?

Put frankly, Thousand Fell is not zero waste.

This is greenwashing.

Hillary Renick defines zero waste as:

  • reducing, reusing, recycling and composting waste locally, where we live, right here, right in this place, where we are now
  • reducing climate pollution by relocalizing the economy
  • transitiong to local, community-centered economies
(Thousand Fell does not do any of that.)

A huge benefit of going zero-waste is eliminating the pollution from transportation.
